Fun Fact:
  • Some words are regular travelers, going on vacation abroad and returning with a new accent. Consider the Arabic dār al-ṣināʿa (literally, house of manufacture; a factory). It was borrowed into Latin around the middle of the previous millenium as arsana, eventually becoming the word we know in English as arsenal. Not quite a place of production, but a place for storage of weapons (“arsenal, n.” OED Online. Oxford University Press.). That same word was borrowed back into Arabic, resulting in tirsana: a place for storage of weapons. So now Arabic has the OG dār al-ṣināʿa as well as its genetically-modified clone, who went abroad for a bit and returned with a funny accent and a new meaning: tirsana.
