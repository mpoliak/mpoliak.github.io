I am a PhD student in Brain and Cognitive Sciences at MIT. My advisor is Ted Gibson. I study psycholinguistics, or what processes in the mind are involed in language production and comprehension. Specifically, I study how we use non-lexical properties of language in comprehension and production. Using the noisy channel framework, I investigate how expectations about the form of the utterance affects how we interpret it. I am also trying to figure out what exactly makes two utterances similar to each other, in speech and in writing, and how this depends on individual differences in linguistic background. Finally, I want to deepen our understanding of how manner of speech (e.g., intonation) shapes the meaning of the utterance. I also love stats, which, back in my freshman year of college, I didn’t believe was possible :)

In the past, as an undergrad at Harvard, I have worked with Anthony Yacovone and Dr. Jesse Snedeker on language development and applying neural decoding to analysis of ERP data. I have also worked with Dr. Mahzarin Banaji for several years on implicit gender-fame stereotyping and implicit attitude acquisition.
